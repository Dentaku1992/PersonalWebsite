Preliminary design of a Flying Wing UAV

  • 2012 - 2013
  • other
  • Gert-Jan Andries and Luca Logier

Overview

In this thesis, I designed a flying wing unmanned aerial vehicle for search and rescue operations above the Belgian North Sea. A mock of the complete electrical system was build as well. This thesis was done in cooperation with VLOC Ostend.

Abstract

The flying wing is a rarely type of plane in aviation and therefore a plane that captures the imagination of many people, partly because the use of flying wings is primarily military oriented. It's difficult to obtain the design of a flying wing, and even more difficult the design of an unmanned flying wing. Therefore, in this thesis we've tried to write out a step by step design process for the design of a flying wing UAV that takes into account the limitations of both, the flying wings and unmanned aircrafts.

The goal is not the design of an everyday unmanned flying wing, but the design of an unmanned flying wing that has the capabilities of a quick intervention plane for operations above the North Sea. With a high availability and a high cruising speed the flying wing can be used when searching for drowning victims, conducting measurements in high risk areas (nuclear disaster) or detecting forest fires. In short, a machine with many possibilities where one thing is common ground, the rapid deployment.

The design process that was used consists out of three essential steps: conceptual, preliminary and detailed design. The requirements for the conceptual design, were given as an input by the KHBO. So the concept was to design a flying wing, powered by an electric ducted fan engine. During the second step, the preliminary design, a preliminary study was done to flying wings and unmanned aerial vehicles. This research gave an idea of ​​the usual relations between flying wings and unmanned aerial vehicles. Next, a mathematical model has been designed as the basis for a first 3D model. During the last phase, the detailed design, all components and systems have been developed in detail. For all components a technical drawing was made. The design was verified by the final result to assess the specific requirements and expectations.
